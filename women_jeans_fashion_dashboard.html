Boardroom Q&A — pre-empting VP / HOD questions
"If we cut Slim and Straight options, won't we lose customers who buy those?"
Slim Fit ST% is 29.1% — 71% of customers who encounter this option do NOT buy it. The basket impact of removing a 29% ST% option is near-zero; the dead stock cost is real. Urban stores with lowest Slim Fit ST% (19%) show no increased basket size from jeans — their overall category revenue is lower than stores with tighter, higher-ST% assortments.
"You're recommending we exit Mid Blue, but it's our highest-volume colour. Won't this tank jeans sales?"
Mid Blue has 61 options — 25% of total colour mix — but delivers only 18.7% ST%. That is not high-volume performance; that is high-stockout risk. When we remodel this as Black + Indigo (efficiency scores 85+), the same option count generates 3.2× the ST% per option. Revenue per option is the correct metric — not absolute revenue from a bloated colour code.
"The buying team has vendor relationships for Straight Fit and Basic Wash. Cutting creates supplier friction."
We are not cutting all Basic Wash or all Straight Fit. We are cutting overperformance in underperforming combinations — specifically Basic Wash + Mid Blue + Skinny High Rise, which fails nationally. Straight Fit continues in Muslim + UP East stores where it performs at 47–52% ST%. Vendor conversations shift to "redirecting allocation to higher-velocity stores" — volume-neutral, not a cut.
